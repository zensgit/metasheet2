## Intro / scope

This document lists **small, concrete humanization micro-improvements** — single fields, toggles, defaults, copy-actions, inline affordances, and confirmation/notification touches — that close the *politeness/ergonomics* gap between MetaSheet attendance and the DingTalk admin experience. Each row traces to a **real manual screen** AND a **real route-inventory result** (a concrete `/api/attendance/...` path, or an honest "not found" where no route matches). This is deliberately **NOT** the big roadmap-arc work: the heavy tracked engines (shift-compliance engine, unscheduled-scheduled reminder engine, punch-policy groups, OT/leave-subtype segmentation, auto-shift-matching) are explicitly **out of scope** here and noted at the end — these rows are intentionally the "last mile" UX polish that sits *beside* those arcs, sized S/M, never a new subsystem. The "MetaSheet today" column is anchored to the endpoint inventory, not keyword-grep counts (e.g. `shiftCompliance`/`enforceShiftEditWindow` exist but govern *schedule editing*, not attendance-*result* editing; `notification-deliveries` is a read-only delivery log, not reminder config).

## Excluded (already-tracked roadmap arcs)

The big benchmark arcs are **out of scope** for this micro-improvement list: the **shift-compliance engine** (`attendance-shift-compliance-engine-design-lock-20260602.md`), the **unscheduled/scheduled reminder engine** (`attendance-unscheduled-reminder-design-lock-20260604.md`), **punch-policy groups** (`attendance-punch-policy-group-design-lock-20260602.md`), **OT/leave-subtype segmentation** (`attendance-overtime-segmentation-engine-design-lock-…`), and **auto-shift-matching** (`attendance-auto-shift-matching-preview-design-lock-…`) — these correspond to the benchmark sequence's larger ④compliance / ⑤OT-leave / ⑥auto-match / reminder / punch tiers and proceed as their own gated, design-locked arcs.
Rows above are deliberately the **last-mile UX polish that sits beside those arcs** (a field, a toggle, a default, a copy-action, an inline gesture) — none of them is a new engine, and rows 1/7/12 are scoped to stay *under* the reminder/edit-window arcs rather than duplicate them.
